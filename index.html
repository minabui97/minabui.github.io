Projects

Geoanalysis of Supporting Services for Current and Future Seniors in Vancouver, 2016

This completed project addressed the risk factors that pose negative impacts on the health and wellbeing of seniors. One of the most significant focuses in public health has been the physical and mental health of seniors, of the most rapidly aging populations in Canada. The goal was to find the gaps in key services across Vancouver neighbourhoods with large populations of vulnerable seniors, which will aid local officials with decision-making pertaining to senior health.

Using Statistics Canada’s 2016 Census and data from the City of Vancouver’s Open Data Catalogue, two maps were created, using ArcGIS to geovisualize these spatial gaps. The spatial outputs include a location map of existing services and seniors and a kernel density map of health, housing, food, and security services versus seniors across Vancouver neighbourhoods.

It was found that although the Hastings-Sunrise, Kensington-Cedar Cottage, Renfrew-Collingwood, and Victoria-Fraserview neighbourhoods contained the highest population of seniors 85 and older, the existing services were clustered in West End, Downtown, Strathcona, Grandview Woodland, Mount Pleasant, and Fairview neighbourhoods. It is recommended to develop more services in these five neighbourhoods in East Vancouver to maximize the support for new retirees and senior citizens in the next five to ten years.

This project was created for GEOG 255: Geographic Information Science I with professor Nicholas Hedley at Simon Fraser University BC in Fall 2018.

Spatial Analysis of 1946 Tokyo, Japan versus Present-Day Tokyo, Japan

This completed paper traced the spatial development to the area of Tokyo, including alterations in the neighbourhoods, towns, cities, land use, infrastructure, the displacement of over a million people, and death to a hundred thousand civilians.

The spatial analysis was supported by an overlay of a 1946 map of bombed-out areas from firebombing air raids during the Pacific campaigns of World War II and a present-day image of Tokyo, Japan, created using Google Earth's Image Overlay.

This project was created for GEOG 377: Environmental History with Professor Timothy Paulsen at Simon Fraser University, Burnaby, BC in Fall 2018.
